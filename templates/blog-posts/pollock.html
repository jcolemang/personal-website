My kid could paint that! It's just paint splattered on the canvas! Where's the skill?! I don't understand Jackson Pollock!

And my goodness, neither do I. I'm a computer scientist! We don't have creativity at all, we just hit 0's and 1's into a terminal until all your passwords fall out.

So then why do I care about Jackson Pollock? Simply put, because I think that just about every one of his paintings looks awfully nice. And I have absolutely no idea why. Because it is just paint plattered on a campus, and theoretically a child absolutely could paint that (although probably not mine, I have no children).

Since my mother first named our splatter colored dog after the famous abstract expressionist I've been intrigued by him and abstract art in general. This interest has largely been fueled by a desire to understand what on Earth the big deal is about a bunch of colors on a canvas. Sure, it's visually pleasing, but so are a million other things that actually look like something. If it doesn't actually look like anything, what's the point? What does it mean? Is there some deep meaning hidden deep in Greyed Rainbow that I missed standing right in front of it? Because it sure didn't look anything like a rainbow, whatever the color scheme.

A picture of Pollock the dog

Well, does the Mona Lisa mean anything? Does The Starry Night? Does The Kiss or Girl With a Pearl Earring or A Sunday Afternoon on the Island of La Grande Jatte? Maybe. Well, probably, and I would be willing to bet that more than one PhD has been given on the meaning of any one of those. But I've never heard anyone complain about them or any other painting "of" something.

My opinion about the most realistic painting "of" something on the planet is this:

Ahem,

My kid could paint that! It's just paint splattered on the canvas! Where's the skill?! I don't understand any artists ever!

Any painting on the planet is just paint splattered on a canvas. That's why it's called a painting. All it is is paint! And just as you could get a team of monkeys to churn out Shakespeare you could get a child to perfectly recreate the Sistine Chapel, the Mona Lisa, or even... a Jackson Pollock painting. If the definition of skill is the theoretical capability to accidentally accomplish something then I should drop out of school now, because all this so called "learning" is really cutting into my random-keys-on-a-typewriter time. I'm writing the next great American novel!

Maybe trying to assign some specific meaning to all art is a silly venture. Maybe the secret to immortality is embedded somewhere in the art of the abstract expressionism movement. Maybe a chocolate chip cookie recipe is embedded instead. Maybe I'm a silly computer nerd who doesn't get it and who tries to write about things he doesn't even slightly understand.

Most likely, though, maybe people who think that abstract art is nonsense should pause for a moment, step back, and say, "doesn't that look nice?"
